# Danny Boy (Londonderry Air)

**Provenance:** The melody "Londonderry Air" is a traditional Irish tune,
first published in George Petrie's "Ancient Music of Ireland," 1855. The
melody is public domain. (Note: the F.E. Weatherly lyrics from 1913 are
still under copyright in some jurisdictions — instrumental performance only
for this songbook.)

**Range:** D4–E5. Stays in C major.

**Why this is intermediate:**
- The tune spans a tenth (D4 to E5), requiring a hand position shift in the
  treble register.
- Phrases are long (8 bars each) — sustaining smooth tone without rushing
  requires breath control in the right arm.

**Practice tips:**
- Play each 8-bar phrase as one long arc. Don't breathe (musically) in the
  middle of a phrase.
- The highest note (E5, bar 9) is the emotional peak — give it a tiny added
  weight.
- Left hand: simple root-fifth on beat 1 and 3, lifting between.
