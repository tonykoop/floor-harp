# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, hole locations, or toolpaths.

## Hero Prompt

A small floor-standing concert-style harp standing on its base on a clean neutral light-gray studio sweep, photographed at a 3/4 product angle with a ~50mm lens. The instrument shows the unmistakable triangular harp silhouette: a curved hardwood neck arcing along the top, a straight forepillar column rising from the base to meet the neck, and a tapering soundbox/soundboard forming the third side. About twenty-two strings fan across the open triangle from short bright treble strings near the top to long bass strings at the bottom, the longest spanning roughly the height of a seated adult's reach so the scale reads as floor-standing rather than lap-sized. Strings are warm translucent nylon and fluorocarbon in the treble grading into darker wound bass strings; a row of tuning pins marches along the neck where each string terminates, and the strings pass through a slim bridge strip down the centerline of the soundboard. The frame is figured hardwood with a hand-rubbed satin finish that catches the light along the curved neck, the soundboard a pale spruce face with subtle grain. Soft directional studio light, gentle falloff, high detail, photorealistic, no text labels.

## In-Context Prompt

The same floor harp staged upright in a warm wood-floored instrument workshop, leaning into playing position beside a low stool, with a window casting soft natural side light across the string fan. Wood shavings, hand tools, and clamps sit softly out of focus in the background, telling the story of a just-finished prototype awaiting its first careful string-up. Shallow depth of field keeps the curved neck and fanned strings crisp while the shop recedes into bokeh. Natural light, environmental storytelling, concept visualization only.

## Detail Prompt

Macro close-up running along the curved hardwood neck where the strings terminate: a row of metal tuning pins set into the neck, each string rising from its pin and angling down into the open frame, treble strings thin and translucent, the transition toward thicker wound bass strings visible at the frame edge. Raking light skims the satin-finished grain of the neck and glints off the pins and string windings, emphasizing the precise repeating geometry of string-to-pin spacing. High detail, shallow depth of field, no measurements.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, or fake maker marks; no dimension callouts or measurement lines; no impossible or floating frame members and no missing pillar; anatomically plausible hands if a player is shown.
